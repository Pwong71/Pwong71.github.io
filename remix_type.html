1A. Project Brief

A transitional font that represents the experience of remembering and forgetting someone.

1B. Intro

“The you I’ve never known” is a phrase I chose based on the theme remember and forget. I will be specifically picking three fonts that represent different stages of knowing and forgetting someone. We often see peoples’ profiles on social media and we try to put the pieces together based on the photos they post and form that person’s personality in our head. My main goal through this project is to represent that superficial and incomplete understanding of a person with the three fonts I picked.

2A. Challenge

To combine 3 different fonts and create a new hybrid version that combines certain typographic characteristics from each.

2B. Finding a font

Before sketching out my remix font, I need to pick out three different fonts to combine. So, I typed out my phrase in 10 fonts that I picked and compared them together.

2C. Examining the fonts

The three fonts I picked are Silk, ZCool and Silom.
Each font represents a stage of knowing and forgetting someone.

Silk: the state when you know a person inside out
Zcool: the transition period between remembering and forgetting someone
Silom: the state when you’re close to forgetting completely but fragmented memories of that person still exist

5. Reflection

Through this project, I’ve struggled a lot with unifying my design. It was very challenging to come up with an uniform approach to every single letter. After at least 5 times of starting over and over again, I decided to write down the biggest problems I've struggled with and came up with a solution and formula for my design. I’ve never done this before since I've always thought that going back to my sketchbook from my laptop is only going to postpone my progress. I was proved wrong and the sketches has helped me drastically.

Picking a bitmap font is also totally out of my comfort zone. But after understanding its special features and controlling the amount I want to incorporate into my final letters, I realized that its characteristics really strengthened my overall design.

It was really satisfying to see my ability to combine three completly different typefaces into one, I also enjoyed adding my personal touch into the final compositions of the posters. Overall, I’ve gained so much knowledge especially on maintaining a consistency and unification on my design through this project. Hope you enjoyed it as much as I enjoyed making it.
