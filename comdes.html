While many schools are becoming more diverse and inclusive, many report not yet having the knowledge in creating a safer environment for LGBTQ2+ youth. Somewhere Over the Rainbow, promotes a highschool seminar regarding how to support LGBTQ2+ youth at school. The campaign includes a promotion poster and a foldable collage zine to give out to students after the seminar.

2d Project focus:
The rainbow flag is known for representing the LGBTQ2 community. The colors symbolize the diversity of the community itself and each color represents a different meaning. My goal is to combine the flag with the infamous song “somewhere over the rainbow” to spread hope, positivity and knowledge on this topic. The message of this campaign is to take the idea of ‘somewhere’ over the rainbow and make it into a vision for this campaign. That ‘somewhere’ will symbolize a safe and loving place for all LGBTQ2+ youth at school if the younger generation can start spreading love and taking action on this issue.
I’ll be creating digital collages to present this idea. Inspirations for this campaign are drawn from nostalgic illustration books and collective childhood memories. Since I’m twisting an old song into a new concept, making collages digitally will achieve a futuristic retro look.
The campaign currently only includes a poster and 8 collages with a word that represents a color of the rainbow flag. I would like to expand on this project by including information about ways to support the LGBTQ2+ community behind each collage to provide readers a deeper understanding on the social issue.
If expanded, the zine would be designed with four spreads on both sides, each spread would consist of a collage and a page of text next to it. With this design, the reader can bring home information about the topic
