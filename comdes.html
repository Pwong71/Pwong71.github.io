1A. Project Brief

A digital collage zine that represents colours of the LGBTQ2+ rainbow flag.

1B. Intro

While many schools are becoming more diverse and inclusive, many reports not having the expertise to build a safer environment for LGBTQ2+ youth. Somewhere Over the Rainbow, promotes a high school seminar regarding how to encourage and support LGBTQ2+ youth at school.

The campaign includes a promotion poster and a foldable collage zine to give out to students following the seminar. The zine consists of 8 pages, one collage per page and one composition for each colour. It allows students to understand the meaning behind each colour of the rainbow flag, which would be a nice piece to bring home.

2D. Project Focus

The rainbow flag is known for representing the LGBTQ2 community. The colours symbolize the diversity of the community itself and each colour expresses a different meaning. My goal is to combine the flag with the famous song “somewhere over the rainbow” to spread hope, positivity and awareness about this issue. The message of this campaign is to take the notion of ‘somewhere’ over the rainbow and make it a vision for this campaign. If the younger generation can start spreading love and taking action on this issue, that 'somewhere' will symbolize a stable and caring place for all LGBTQ2+ youth at school.

2E. Current perception

There is certainly a lack of education at schools on the subject of the LGBTQ2+ community. Teenagers have a tendency to listen to their peers at a young age, their thinking patterns are also highly influenced by the knowledge they absorb and receive on a regular basis. Being deceived by prejudices and the biases of other people can affect their perceptions of many important topics. Young adolescents appear to see the LGBTQ2+ culture as an entire category of individuals without understanding what struggles they are going through.

2F. Desired perception

My goal for this campaign is to not only educate teenagers on what this community is all about, but also focus on showing them the positivity, beauty and energy of this group of individuals, understand and find out what daily struggles LGBTQ2+ youths are going through and learn how to bring more awareness and attention to this issue.

5. Reflection

The campaign currently only includes a poster and 8 collages with a word that represents a colour of the rainbow flag. I would like to expand on this project by including information about ways to support the LGBTQ2+ community behind each collage to provide readers a deeper understanding of this social issue. If expanded, the zine would be designed with four spreads on both sides, each spread would consist of a collage and a page of text next to it. With this design, the reader can bring home more in-depth information regarding this topic.
